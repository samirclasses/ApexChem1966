Principle of Atom Conservation (POAC)

Principle

The Principle of Atom Conservation (POAC) is a powerful tool in stoichiometry used to solve chemical calculation problems, especially when the complete chemical equation is unknown or too complex to balance.

It is based on the Law of Conservation of Mass, which states that matter can neither be created nor destroyed. In a chemical reaction, the total number of atoms of each element remains constant.

Core Concept

Atoms are rearranged during reactions but not created or destroyed:

Moles of an atom in Reactants = Moles of that same atom in Products

Mathematical Formula

If A is present in AxBy:

Moles of atom A = x × (Moles of compound AxBy)

How to Apply POAC

  1. Identify the element
  2. Write POAC equation
  3. Substitute mole values
  4. Solve

Example

Problem: How many grams of KClO3 produce 48 g O2?

3 × (moles of KClO3) = 2 × (moles of O2)

3 × (w / 122.5) = 2 × (48 / 32)

w = 122.5 g

Limitations

  • No mechanism information
  • Cannot predict feasibility
  • No energy details
  • Not valid for nuclear reactions
