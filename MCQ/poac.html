Principle of Atom Conservation (POAC)

Principle

The Principle of Atom Conservation states that in any chemical reaction, atoms are neither created nor destroyed. The number of atoms of each element remains constant throughout the reaction, ensuring that mass is conserved.

In simple terms: Total atoms before reaction = Total atoms after reaction.

Examples

Example 1:
2H2 + O2 → 2H2O
Hydrogen atoms: 4 → 4
Oxygen atoms: 2 → 2

Example 2:
CH4 + 2O2 → CO2 + 2H2O
Carbon: 1 → 1
Hydrogen: 4 → 4
Oxygen: 4 → 4

Example 3:
CaCO3 → CaO + CO2
Calcium: 1 → 1
Carbon: 1 → 1
Oxygen: 3 → 3

Limitations

  • Does not provide information about reaction mechanism.
  • Cannot predict reaction feasibility.
  • Does not indicate energy changes in reactions.
  • Applies only to chemical reactions, not nuclear reactions.
