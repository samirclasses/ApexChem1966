Slater's Rule

Interactive Guide to Effective Nuclear Charge (Zeff)

1. Effective Nuclear Charge

The effective nuclear charge experienced by an electron is

Zeff = Z − S

where

  • Z = Atomic number
  • S = Shielding constant calculated using Slater's Rules

2. Slater's Rules

Electron Type Shielding Contribution
Other electrons in same 1s group 0.30 each
Other electrons in same ns or np group 0.35 each
(n−1) shell for ns/np electron 0.85 each
(n−2) or lower shells 1.00 each
For nd or nf electron: same nd/nf group 0.35 each
All electrons to the left 1.00 each
Electrons to the right 0

3. Worked Examples

Example 1: 1s electron of Helium (Z=2)

Other 1s electron contributes = 0.30

S = 0.30

Zeff = 2 − 0.30 = 1.70

Example 2: 2p electron of Nitrogen (Z=7)

Configuration: 1s² 2s² 2p³

Same shell (4 electrons): 4 ×0.35 =1.40

1s electrons: 2 ×0.85 =1.70

S=3.10

Zeff=7−3.10=3.90

Example 3: 3d electron of Iron (Z=26)

Configuration: [Ar]3d⁶4s²

Other 3d electrons=5×0.35=1.75

All electrons to left=18×1=18

4s electrons do NOT shield the 3d electron.

S=19.75

Zeff=26−19.75=6.25

Example 4: 4f electron (Lanthanide)

Same 4f electrons contribute 0.35 each.

All electrons to the left contribute 1.00 each.

Electrons outside the 4f orbital contribute zero shielding.
